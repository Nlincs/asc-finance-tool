Eligible for some financial support

You will have to pay a contribution to the cost of your care from your income, plus an extra amount depending on your capital

How do we work out how much your contribution will be?

We follow a simple 4 step process.

Example

Miss Green is 83 she lives on her own. She receives a direct payment of £180.00 per week from the council. She has the following capital and savings:

£15,000 - Lloyds bank accounts

£3,500 - Post Office Account

£18,500 - Total Capital

As Miss Green has capital over £14,250, capital tariff of £1 for every £250 of capital over £14,250 will be included as part of her income, calculated as follows:

£18,500 - £14,250 = £4,250

£4,250 / £250 = £17.00.

Miss Green incurs capital tariff of £17.00 per week.

  1. Step 1 Add together all of your different income

    List the different sources of income. Here is an example:

    1. Income

    2. £147.00 per week - State Retirement Pension

    3. £13.02 per week - British Steel Pension

    4. £48.00 per week - Co-op Pension

    5. £57.30 per week - Attendance Allowance (Low rate)

    6. £17.00 per week - Capital Tariff (as calculated above)

    7. £282.32 per week - Total Weekly Income

  2. Step 2 Add up any income we don't count

    There are some things we exclude, for example:

    1. Discounts and allowances

    2. £21.60 per week - Housing Related Expenditure

    3. £3.00 per week - Disability Related Expenditure

    4. £203.75 per week - Income Support +25%

    5. £228.35 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

  3. Step 3 Calculate total income that can be used for contributions

    Take the total weekly discounts and allowances from the total weekly income.

    1. £282.32 per week - Total Weekly Income

    2. -£228.35 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

    3. £53.97 per week - Weekly contribution

  4. Step 4 Compare the assessed income with the total cost of care

    We then compare the total assessed income (£53.97 per week), with the total cost of services (£180.00 per week). Miss. Green would pay whichever amount is lower.

    She will pay £53.97 per week toward the cost of her care. The council will pay the remaining cost of her service of £126.03 directly to Miss Green.

What happens next

If you are having, or have already had a Care Assessment and want to know exactly how much you will pay for services or you want to apply for financial support, you can use the BetterCare tool to complete a Financial Assessment.
