Eligible for financial support

You will have to pay a contribution to the cost of your care, but only from your income

How do we work out how much your contribution will be?

  1. Step 1 Add together all of your different income

    Many people only have one source of income, such as their State Pension, but if you have other sources of income we will take them into account.

    These may include:

    • state pension
    • wages/salary
    • private/work pensions
    • rental income from tenants and boarders
    • maintenance payments from a spouse/partner

    Income from benefits is usually included. We may ask you to apply for benefits to which you are entitled but have not yet claimed, to reduce the burden on council budgets.

    • Attendance Allowance, including Constant Attendance Allowance and Exceptionally Severe Disablement Allowance
    • Bereavement Support Payment (has replaced Bereavement Allowance, previously Widow’s Pension)
    • Carer's Allowance
    • Child Tax Credit
    • Child Benefit
    • Disability Living Allowance (Care component)
    • Employment and Support Allowance, or the benefits this replaces such as Severe Disablement Allowance and Incapacity Benefit
    • Income Support
    • Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit or equivalent benefits
    • Jobseeker’s Allowance
    • Maternity Allowance
    • Pension Credit
    • Personal Independence Payment (Daily Living component)
    • Universal Credit
    • Working Tax Credit

  2. Step 2 Take away allowances and discounts

    There are some things we exclude, for example:

    Household Expenditure Costs

      1. £13.40 per week - Pension Credit (Savings Credit)

      2. £28.30 per week - Attendance Allowance

      3. £7.05 per week - Disability Related Expenditure

      4. £203.75 per week - Income Support +25%

      5. £252.50 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

      Discounts and allowances

        1. £13.40 per week - Pension Credit (Savings Credit)

        2. £28.30 per week - Attendance Allowance

        3. £7.05 per week - Disability Related Expenditure

        4. £203.75 per week - Income Support +25%

        5. £252.50 per week - Total Weekly Discounts and allowances

  3. Step 3 Compare the assessed income with the total cost of care

    We then compare the total assessed income (£73.80), with the total cost of services (£55.00). Mr. Jenkins would pay whichever amount is lower.

    He will pay £55.00 per week, but if the cost of Mr. Jenkins services was to increase in the future, he would pay no more than £73.80 per week in contributions.

What happens next

If you are having, or have already had a Care Assessment and want to know exactly how much you will pay for services or you want to apply for financial support, you can use the BetterCare tool to complete a Financial Assessment.
